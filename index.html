“Happiness is the meaning and the purpose of life, the whole aim and end of human existence.”

These are the words of Aristotle, and his utterance holds even today. The value in happiness is global, but it is safe to say that all humans have their emotional ups and downs. Sometimes we feel happy and sometimes we feel low, but have you ever wondered

where the happiest people in the world live?
Turns out they seem to live up in the North!




The World Happiness Report has investigated happiness across the globe annually since 2012. It ranks the countries' and for the last two years Finland has been in the very top, followed by its neighbors Denmark and Norway. Sweden hits the list on 7th place.

So is it safe to say that my happiness depends on where I live?
The short answer is: yes, to some extent. But there are also other important factors...

Can money buy happiness?

Turns out it can - to a certain extent

Exactly, how much do you need to earn annually to be happy?

~$75.000
for emotional well-being
$95.000
for life evaluation satisfaction

‘Money money money, must be funny in a rich man’s world’ is the lyrics in ABBA’s famous song. How much money is fun though? Research show that, on a global level, individuals with an annual income of between $60,000 - $75,000 are likely to reach satiation of emotional well-being, referring to the emotional quality of an individual’s everyday experience. An income of $95,000 will likely make you feel satiation on life evaluation, referring to the thoughts that people have about their life when they think about it.

So if money really can buy happiness,
is there a ceiling to how happy you can get for money?
According to researchers: Yes, there is - It turns out that as your annual income rise above $95,000, the happiness curve tend to flatten out, or even fall. Meaning that making more money could eventually steal your happiness from you. So a more accurate melody of ABBA’s hit would be
‘Money money money, is only fun up to a certain threshold in an individuals world’


What can I do to increase my happiness?

So if I don’t have the possibility to neither raise my salary or leave the country - is there anything else I, as an individual, can do in order to change my happily ever after destiny?

Of course there is:
you can aim to work less!
As contradictory to the data presented above as it sounds, as working more most likely will result in a larger income, which could contribute to a higher level of happiness, research also show a correlation between decreased working load and increased happiness. Not to surprising though, as working less naturally should give you more time for other activities important for your well-being and happiness, such as spending time with family and friends, which has also shown to increase happines.
